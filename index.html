A study of 356 finance managers across Nepal's commercial banks, development banks, finance companies, and microfinance institutions, showing that investment decisions partially explain how liquidity and risk shape dividend payouts.

About

Background

I'm a researcher affiliated with Nepal College of Management, Kathmandu University, currently completing a Master's in Economics. My background is in empirical corporate finance — testing how financial variables relate to each other using survey data and structural equation modeling, with a particular focus on Nepal's banking sector.

Alongside research, I've worked as a research assistant tracking macroeconomic indicators and supporting investment analysis, and interned in foreign exchange licensing at Nepal Rastra Bank.
