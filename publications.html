The International Research Journal of Management Science  ·  Vol. 9, Issue 1  ·  December 2024  ·  pp. 135–150

Impact of Liquidity and Risk on Dividend Policy: A Mediating Role of Investment

Prem Prasad Silwal, Roshan KC, Phadindra Kumar Paudel

This study looks at how liquidity and risk shape dividend policy in Nepal's banking sector, and specifically whether a bank's investment decisions sit in between those two forces and the payout it eventually gives shareholders. Data came from a questionnaire sent to finance managers, CFOs, and finance directors across commercial banks, development banks, finance companies, and microfinance institutions, with 356 usable responses analyzed using structural equation modeling in AMOS.

The results show that both liquidity and risk have a direct, positive effect on dividend policy, and that a meaningful part of that effect runs indirectly through investment: banks with more liquidity or greater risk exposure tend to funnel that into profitable investments, which in turn supports higher payouts down the line. The mediation held for both variables, though it was somewhat stronger for risk than for liquidity.
